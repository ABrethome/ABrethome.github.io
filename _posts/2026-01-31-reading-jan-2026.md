**Title: Don’t Use LLMs as OCR: Lessons Learned from Extracting Complex Documents**

[Link](https://medium.com/@martia_es/dont-use-llms-as-ocr-lessons-learned-from-extracting-complex-documents-db2d1fafcdfb)

Summary: The article argues against using LLMs as OCR for complex documents, especially when numerical accuracy matters. Experiments showed LLMs often misread numbers in scanned PDFs, charts, and tables, making them unreliable for faithful extraction. The author found that combining traditional OCR (for accurate structure and data extraction) with LLMs only for formatting, reasoning, and restructuring delivers far more reliable results.

Key learnings applicable to my RAG pipelines:
- Separate extraction from interpretation: use deterministic OCR for text, tables, and numbers; reserve LLMs for semantic enrichment and transformation.
- Never trust LLMs with critical numbers: even small digit errors can cascade into severe downstream issues.
- Normalize before chunking: generating a faithful intermediate format (e.g. Markdown) improves searchability and chunk quality.
- Use LLM vision descriptively, not extractively: describing graphs/images works best after ground‑truth content is secured

**Title: Agentic AI Evaluation Strategy & Metrics**

[Link](https://ai.gopubby.com/agentic-ai-evaluation-strategy-15db5e554180?gi=f37763368133)

Summary: Key learnings for evaluating agents in production:
- Evaluation is not just a development-time activity; it must also run continuously in production to detect drift, off‑script behavior, and quality regressions. Evals underpin trust, rollback, and safe scaling.
- Agent quality depends on model choice, planning logic, memory, tool orchestration, and enterprise constraints. Generic LLM benchmarks or leaderboards are insufficient for real agentic use cases.
- Combine domain‑specific benchmarks, LLM‑as‑a‑Judge for scalable scoring, and selective human review. Each alone is incomplete; together they balance cost, speed, and reliability.
- Measure reasoning relevance and coherence, groundedness, task decomposition efficiency, robustness, consistency, and error recovery; not just final output correctness.
- Tool selection accuracy, parameter precision, call success rate, and unnecessary calls are critical metrics for agent reliability and cost control.
- Log‑based offline evaluation enables non‑invasive scoring at scale, while real‑time evals support live guardrails, alerts, and automated intervention. I need both.
- Evaluation frameworks should explicitly detect risks such as goal misalignment, tool misuse, memory poisoning, cascading hallucinations, and bias—before incidents surface externally.

**Title: Build a Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Transformer from Scratch**

[Link](https://www.intoai.pub/p/build-a-mixture-of-experts-transformer?utm_source=post-email-title&publication_id=1813260&post_id=184419011&utm_campaign=email-post-title&isFreemail=true&r=54qi6w&triedRedirect=true)

Summary: The article explains how to build a Mixture‑of‑Experts (MoE) Transformer to scale models efficiently. It replaces the dense feed‑forward layer with multiple expert MLPs and a learned routing (gating) function that activates only a subset of experts per token. This sparse activation increases parameter count without proportional compute, improves specialization, and discusses key design choices like top‑k routing, load balancing losses, and training stability.
